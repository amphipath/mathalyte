raison d'être

mathalyte is a blog which aims to be a useful resource for everyone who isn't breezing through every math test, providing articles that go further in-depth into the art of problem solving. It hopes to be the the crackling brûlée on top of the rich, lasciviously creamy custard foundation of most mathematics syllabuses in the world.

What mathalyte won't do for you

  • mathalyte won't be your textbook. We're not dealing with homework here; we're dealing with thought processes. The best way for you to take something away from this is to hop along for the ride yourself and think carefully about the questions you ask yourself to inch yourself closer to the answer. Think about why you don't ask yourself said questions, or even think about what your first thoughts would be in the same situation instead. You know you're really thinking when you stop multiple times in the middle of an article to really internalise what's going on.

  • mathalyte won't be your cheatsheet. Congratulations to you if one of the example questions I talk about here happens to be exactly the question you're stuck on; it's bound to happen (there's a rather old adage that there's no such thing as a new mathematics question). But rather than running away with that quick fix, how about you stick around and hopefully learn how to come up with your own answers so that you won't get stuck on other questions in the future?

  • mathalyte won't help you practice. As much as I believe in the value of thinking when it comes to mathematics, when it comes to standardised tests practice helps a hell lot too. Unfortunately, I have neither the resources nor the will to dedicate my time to getting millions of practice questions.

What mathalyte will do for you

  • mathalyte will help you get good. In Dota 2, CS:GO or basically any other decent competitive game, you get good by playing more. But skill isn't tied to your hours played in Steam; it comes from gradually learning to make the right decisions in certain situations and learning to anticipate. Yes, mathalyte aims to be the shortcut to the metagame of most math exams.

  • mathalyte will talk about mental habits you should have when approaching a particularly challenging problem. Questions you should ask yourself. Ways of looking at problems with a different angle. General tips & tricks you should keep in mind. With all candor, I admit that this is quite the unrealistic aim; it's still an aim regardless.
